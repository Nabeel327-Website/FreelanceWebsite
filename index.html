About the Project

This platform helps students improve employability by learning how to build a professional portfolio and start freelancing with confidence.

Project Aim

To design and develop a digital freelancing and portfolio development website that enhances student employability by providing structured guidance, practical tools, and useful resources within a single platform.

Objectives

  • Develop a responsive and user-friendly website.
  • Create at least five portfolio templates.
  • Provide structured freelancing guidance sections.
  • Support students with proposal-writing tips.
  • Collect usability feedback from students.

Freelancing Starter Checklist

  • Choose one service area linked to your existing skills.
  • Write a short, clear professional summary.
  • Prepare 2–3 sample projects for your portfolio.
  • Use simple proposal wording and avoid overpromising.
  • Collect feedback and improve your profile gradually.

Students can begin with services connected to their current abilities such as graphic design, content writing, social media support, web design, basic coding, video editing, tutoring, or CV design.

A good profile should include a clear headline, concise summary, key skills, education, examples of work, and a professional tone. Students should focus on clarity, honesty, and relevance.

Strong proposals should show understanding of the task, explain how the student can help, highlight relevant skills, and use clear and respectful communication.

A portfolio should contain project titles, descriptions, screenshots, tools used, personal contributions, and outcomes. Even academic work can be adapted into professional portfolio evidence.

Students improve employability when they can demonstrate practical skills, communication, initiative, and digital professionalism. A portfolio and freelancing knowledge help strengthen these areas.
